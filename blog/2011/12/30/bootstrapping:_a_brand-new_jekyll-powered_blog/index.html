bootstrapping: a brand-new jekyll-powered blog

Published on 30-12-2011 in [ jekyll ]

I recently decided to move my custom-made personal homepage and my blogger-powered blog onto a single website. I stumbled upon jekyll, a "blog aware, static site generator". It can be freely hosted on github, via github-pages. There is no database and every page and post are written in markdown (or another outlining language). The source is located in a folder on my site and are controlled via git. If you want a similar website, you can clone the repo and look for the user-contributed plugins.

Three jekyll bloggers, among the others, inspired me for building this site:

  • stammy
  • josé diaz gonsalez
  • kevin sornberger
  • lukencode from which I took most of the theme

You may also have a look at other static html generators, such as pelican, nanoblogger or hyde. I have tested the first two, they were great but jekyll is better (in terms of plugins, community and the bonus of github hosting).

The only issue I still have to figure out is how to neatly publish posts in French and English. The authors of sp4ce.net and drivemeup have kind of succeeded in doing this, but I need some time to see how I can adapt their solutions to this blog.

However, jekyll and github are really great!
